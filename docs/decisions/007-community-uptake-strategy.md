# 007 — Community uptake: partnership-first with Orca Network, targeting the shore regular

**Status:** proposed (direction chosen; blocked on partner discovery) · **Decided:** 2026-06 (strategy note)

## Decision

The path to first-party submissions runs through a **partnership with Orca Network**, targeting the **shore regular** persona. The real competitor is Orca Network's real-time sighting-coordination feed inside Facebook; the wedge is doing that coordination function properly (map-first, structured, trajectory-aware). Lead with the partnership, not a scraping hack.

Full reasoning: [docs/strategy/community-uptake.md](../strategy/community-uptake.md).

## The two offers

1. Delete Orca Network's manual Facebook-to-report transcription chore, and give their community a better field tool.
2. Tell the research-impact story: community observations already feed science (Acartia → WRAS → researchers), but that plumbing is invisible today. Making it visible is unsolved and differentiated.

## Rejected

- Cross-post / bridge-both-ways with Facebook (concedes the coordination function).
- Newcomer-onboarding-first (wrong persona; shore regulars carry the signal).
- Scraping as the lead move (burns the partnership; kept only as fallback).

## Status and sequencing

This direction rests on a partner we don't yet understand. **Next move is discovery, not engineering** — the interview questions are at [docs/strategy/orca-network-discovery-questions.md](../strategy/orca-network-discovery-questions.md). The "make the sighter's contribution visible" work should sequence after the map is populated and after the Orca Network direction is confirmed.

Prize sizing: ~1,150 reporters/year (¾ first-timers), 15k email subscribers, ~140k FB followers.
